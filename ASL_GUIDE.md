# American Sign Language (ASL) Guide

## What is American Sign Language?

American Sign Language is a complete, natural language that has the same structure as any spoken language. It is used primarily by Deaf and hard of hearing people in the United States and some parts of Canada.

## Basic ASL Principles

### 1. Hand Shapes (Handforms)

- Each sign begins with a specific hand shape
- There are over 60 different basic handforms
- Examples: A-shape, C-shape, F-shape, L-shape, etc.

### 2. Movement (Motion)

- How the hand moves through space
- Can be: straight, circular, bouncing, twisting, etc.
- Speed and repetition matter

### 3. Location (Place of Articulation)

- Where the sign is made: near face, body, or in neutral space
- Can be: head, face, neck, torso, hands, arm, etc.

### 4. Orientation (Palm Direction)

- Which way the palm faces during the sign
- Can be: up, down, forward, backward, or sideways

### 5. Non-Manual Markers (NMM)

- Facial expressions, head movements, body tilts
- These modify meaning and grammar
- Examples: raised eyebrows for questions, head nods for affirmation

## Hand Shapes - The 26 Alphabet

ASL fingerspelling uses hand positions for each letter A-Z:

```
A - Fist with thumb to side
B - Open hand, fingers together, palm forward
C - Curved open hand, like holding a cup
D - Index up, other fingers curled
E - All fingers bent inward
F - Thumb and index curve, others extended
G - Index and middle separated
H - Index and middle separated, side-by-side
I - Pinky extended
J - Pinky curves downward
K - Like H with middle finger extended up
L - Index and thumb extended
M - Three middle fingers bent over thumb
N - Two middle fingers bent over thumb
O - All fingers curved, touching thumb
P - Like F with middle finger down
Q - Like P with slight rotation
R - Index and middle crossed
S - Fist with thumb on side
T - Thumb between index and middle
U - Index and middle extended together
V - Index and middle in V shape
W - Index, middle, ring extended in W shape
X - Index curled, crosses other fingers
Y - Thumb and pinky extended
Z - Index finger traces Z shape
```

## Sign Recognition Tips

### For Signers

- **Use two hands when needed**: Many signs use both hands
- **Face the camera directly**: Avoid turning sideways
- **Maintain distance**: 2-3 feet is optimal
- **Use space**: Signs happen in a signing space around your body
- **Clear movement**: Don't rush, make deliberate movements
- **Facial expressions**: Part of sign grammar

### For Best App Recognition

- ✅ Ensure good front lighting
- ✅ Plain background behind you
- ✅ Keep hands above shoulder height
- ✅ Make complete, defined gestures
- ✅ Move slowly and clearly
- ✅ One sign at a time
- ✅ Let recognition complete between signs

## ASL Grammar Basics

### Word Order

- Topic + Comment (Often starts with topic: "Book, I read")
- Questions at end of sentence
- Statements end with shoulder drop

### Tense

- Indicated through time signs and spatial markers
- Past: "recent past" vs. "distant past"
- Future: Point forward or upward
- Continuous: Hold sign, repeat movement

### Agreement & Spatial Reference

- Use space to establish locations
- Refer back to established locations
- Pronouns change based on spatial location

## Common Phrases

### Introduction

"Hello, my name is [fingerspell name]"

- HELLO (wave)
- MY NAME (point to self, then spell)
- [FINGERSPELL NAME]

### Asking How Someone Is

"How are you?"

- HOW + YOU (point)
- Raised eyebrows for question

### Saying Nice to Meet You

"Nice to meet you"

- NICE (open hand across body)
- MEET (both hands come together)
- YOU (point)

### Simple Conversation

"What is your name?"

- WHAT + YOUR + NAME
- Raised eyebrows

## Respect & Etiquette

When learning or using ASL:

1. **Ask permission** - Ask Deaf individuals if they're willing to sign with you
2. **Be patient** - Learning takes time and practice
3. **Don't assume** - Not all deaf people know ASL
4. **Use names** - Deaf people have sign names
5. **Face direction** - Always face the person you're signing to
6. **Maintain attention** - Don't look away while signing
7. **Respect culture** - ASL is tied to Deaf culture
8. **Keep learning** - Continuous improvement is important

## App-Specific Signs

This app currently recognizes these ASL signs using hand shape detection:

1. **Hello** - Open hand with fingers spread, waving motion
2. **Thank You** - Both hands together near chest, moving forward
3. **Yes** - Fist with thumb extended upward (thumbs up)
4. **Please** - Flat hand rubbing chest in circular motion
5. **Good** - Thumbs up gesture with fist
6. **A** - Fist with thumb tucked inside curled fingers
7. **B** - Flat hand with all fingers extended together
8. **C** - Hand curved in C-shape, thumb and pinky close
9. **I** - Pinky finger extended, other fingers curled
10. **Love** - Both hands crossed over heart area

**How it works**: The app uses MediaPipe to detect 21 hand landmarks (finger joints, palm) and analyzes finger positions to recognize these specific hand shapes and gestures.
